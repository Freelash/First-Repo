Bodies of Water

Ocean

The Ocean is the largest body of water, it is a body of saltwater, covering about 71% of the Earth's surface, and is typically divided into five major areas.

Lake

large body of still water, seperate from the Ocean. They are surrounded by land, and can be either freshwater or saltwater.

River

A river is a natural stream of flowing water that moves downhill due to gravity. It can vary in size, being wide and deep or shallow enough to wade across. Rivers flow in channels with defined banks and can eventually lead to larger bodies of water, such as oceans or lakes.
